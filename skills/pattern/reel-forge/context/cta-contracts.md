## CTA Rules
source: reel-forge/SKILL.md

**HORMOZI:** Exactly one action. "Comment X", "DM me Y", or "Click link." Never two actions in one CTA. The action must be frictionless — comment > DM > link (ascending friction order; pick the lowest that serves the goal).

**NAVAL:** Zero CTA. The conversion mechanism is trust-through-density — the viewer saves the Reel. Adding a CTA to a NAVAL SCRIPT breaks the voice contract and signals inauthenticity. If the creator insists, switch to BUILDER voice.

**BUILDER:** One soft CTA allowed. "If this helped, save it" or "Share this with someone who needs it." Never a transactional CTA ("buy", "sign up") — BUILDER credibility comes from the story, not the offer.

**CONTRARIAN:** One soft CTA or none. "Tell me what you think in the comments" is the strongest CTA that fits — it invites disagreement, which is on-brand for CONTRARIAN voice.

**GODIN:** None or a community CTA. "If you've felt this, you're not alone — follow for more" is the ceiling. Never a transactional CTA.

**JOBS:** One product/reveal CTA. "Try it at [url]" or "Available now." Delivered with the same theatrical certainty as the reveal. No hedging in the CTA line.

**Global CTA rules:**
- CTA is always the last line of the SCRIPT. Never embedded mid-script.
- Never two CTAs in one SCRIPT regardless of VOICE.
- on-screen text for CTA: show the action word only ("COMMENT", "SAVE", "DM") — not the full CTA sentence.
