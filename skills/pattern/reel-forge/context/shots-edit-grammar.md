## Shot Grammar
source: reel-forge/SKILL.md

**Primary shot: talking-head**
Single camera, eye-level or slightly below. Creator fills 60-70% of frame. Background: clean or intentionally contextual (home office, outdoor, gym — matches the VOICE energy).
- NAVAL: static shot, no movement.
- HORMOZI: can use a slight push-in during the result line.
- GODIN: medium shot, slightly more headroom — creates intimacy without closeness.
- BUILDER/CONTRARIAN: tight talking-head for the HOOK, can pull back slightly for the mechanism.

**B-roll (use sparingly):**
Only for HORMOZI and BUILDER when the proof requires visual evidence (screenshots, results, tools). NAVAL and GODIN: never B-roll. B-roll that decorates rather than proves is a distraction.

**on-screen text placement:**
- Upper third: supporting claim or HOOK keyword.
- Lower third: CTA text only.
- Center: only for single-word impact overlays (the CONTRARIAN belief being contradicted).
Never cover the creator's face with text.

## Edit Grammar
source: reel-forge/SKILL.md

**Cut on energy, not on time:**
Cut when the creator's energy peaks on a word — not at a fixed interval. HORMOZI: cut on the key number. CONTRARIAN: cut on the belief word. NAVAL: no cut mid-sentence; cut only between aphorisms.

**Jump cuts:**
Acceptable for BUILDER and HORMOZI where pace matters. Not for NAVAL or GODIN where stillness is the signal.

**Caption timing:**
On-screen text appears 0.5s after the word is spoken. Auto-captions: always on (accessibility + muted viewing). Caption color: white with dark outline for readability on any background.

**Music:**
NAVAL: ambient, low volume, no beat. HORMOZI: none or bass-heavy background at very low volume. GODIN: ambient or none. BUILDER/CONTRARIAN: optional, energetic but non-distracting. Music never competes with the SCRIPT.

**Hook cut:**
First edit cut happens before 3 seconds — typically after the HOOK's concrete specific has landed. This signals to the algorithm that the video is structured and retains viewers past the opening.
