## Voice Profiles
source: reel-forge/SKILL.md

**HORMOZI**
Argument structure: Proof before promise. Result → mechanism → single-action CTA. Every claim is quantified. Every sentence is short and load-bearing. Energy is high but controlled — urgency without panic. Signature pattern: "[Number] in [time]. Here's how." Forbidden: hedging, "I think", multiple CTAs, philosophical framing without proof.

**NAVAL**
Argument structure: Aphorism stack. Each line is a complete, standalone truth. No steps, no proof burden, no CTA. Energy is low, pace is slow. The SCRIPT is saveable as individual quotes. Signature pattern: Reframe a common idea into its opposite at higher resolution. Forbidden: CTAs, listicles, urgency, tactical steps.

**GODIN**
Argument structure: Story → empathy → worldview shift. Opens with a specific scene or person. Closes with the audience seeing themselves differently. Energy is quiet and inviting. No listicles, no bullet structures. Signature pattern: "This is a story about [person]. What they really wanted was [truth]." Forbidden: tactics, urgency, hard CTAs, the word "tips."

**JOBS**
Argument structure: Context → contrast → reveal. Sets up what existed before, names the gap, delivers the reveal as if inevitable. Energy builds from flat to theatrical at the reveal. Signature pattern: "[Old thing] was [limitation]. Today, [the reveal]." Forbidden: hedging, qualifiers, "it depends", anything that softens the reveal.

**BUILDER**
Argument structure: Personal failure → mechanism → earned principle. The HOOK is a specific admission (number, loss, mistake). Credibility comes from having lived the lesson, not having theorized it. Energy is conversational at the HOOK, builds through the mechanism. Signature pattern: "I [specific failure] so you don't have to." Forbidden: Generic failures ("I made mistakes"), unearned confidence, lessons the creator did not personally experience.

**CONTRARIAN**
Argument structure: Named belief → refutation → correct mechanism. The HOOK names the audience's belief before contradicting it. Credibility comes from specificity — the more precisely the belief is named, the more trust the refutation earns. Energy is calm confidence, not aggression. Signature pattern: "[Belief most creators hold] is wrong. Here's what actually works." Forbidden: Vague targets ("most people think"), refutation without a correct mechanism, anger or condescension.
