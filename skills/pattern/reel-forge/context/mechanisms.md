## Core Mechanisms
source: reel-forge/SKILL.md

**M1. Curiosity gap (HOOK → hold)**
Trigger: HOOK opens with a claim that violates the viewer's existing belief (CONTRARIAN) or reveals a surprising result (HORMOZI, BUILDER).
Mechanism: The cognitive gap between what the viewer expected and what was stated activates information-seeking behavior — they stay to close the gap.
Observable result: Watch time extends past 3 seconds; the SCRIPT must pay off the gap with a concrete mechanism or the viewer exits at the flip point (~30-45s).

**M2. Argument structure transfer (VOICE → credibility)**
Trigger: The SCRIPT holds one VOICE throughout (HORMOZI proof-first, NAVAL aphorism stack, CONTRARIAN reversal).
Mechanism: Consistent argument structure signals a coherent worldview. The viewer attributes the structure's credibility (proof = trustworthy; aphorism = wise; reversal = courageous) to the creator.
Observable result: Save rate increases. Blending two structures cancels both — the viewer cannot attribute a consistent worldview and trusts neither.

**M3. Concrete specificity (anchor → recall)**
Trigger: First line of HOOK contains a number, named tool, place, or admission.
Mechanism: Concrete specifics activate episodic memory encoding — the viewer links the claim to a real-world anchor rather than a category. Generic claims are processed categorically and forgotten.
Observable result: Comment engagement references the specific ("47 clients", "Nepal", "$2k/day") rather than the category. Saves and shares increase because the specific is the shareable unit.

**M4. Fast-lane default (no negotiation → shipping)**
Trigger: Input arrives without a named VOICE or format choice.
Mechanism: auto-match selects the VOICE from topic signal and fires the pre-wired recipe. No mode pick means no decision tax before the SCRIPT.
Observable result: DONE output is produced in one pass. Sessions that negotiate mode first produce fewer shipped reels (the 22-module trap — SKILL.md).

**M5. CTA contract (VOICE → action constraint)**
Trigger: SCRIPT reaches the close.
Mechanism: Each VOICE has a fixed CTA rule derived from its credibility source. HORMOZI: one action (comment/DM). NAVAL: none (trust via density). BUILDER/CONTRARIAN: one soft CTA or none. Violating the contract breaks the argument structure's credibility signal.
Observable result: A NAVAL SCRIPT with a CTA reads as inauthentic; audience recognizes the mismatch and trust drops. Correct enforcement means the close feels inevitable, not transactional.

**M6. Open loop paid at the flip (structure → retention)**
Trigger: HOOK creates a promise; SCRIPT reaches the 30-45s mark (the "flip point").
Mechanism: The audience's tension peaks at the flip and resolves only if the promised mechanism arrives. Paying the loop at the flip (not at the end) sustains attention through the close.
Observable result: Retention graphs show a hold at the flip rather than a drop. Eval task "foundation-open-loop-paid-at-flip" confirms the [30-45s] timestamp is load-bearing.
