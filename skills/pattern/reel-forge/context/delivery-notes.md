## Delivery Notes
source: reel-forge/SKILL.md

**HORMOZI talking-head delivery:**
- Fast pace, clipped sentences. No filler words.
- Lean slightly forward on the result line ([0-3s]).
- Hard pause after the key number. Let it land.
- CTA delivered at normal speed, single action, no apologizing.
- Forbidden: "um", "kind of", "you know", "I think."

**NAVAL talking-head delivery:**
- Slow, deliberate pace. Long pauses between aphorisms.
- Eye contact held through the full line — no glancing away mid-sentence.
- No physical gesturing. Stillness signals density.
- Each line delivered as if it is the last thing you will say.
- on-screen text: none. The words carry alone.

**BUILDER talking-head delivery:**
- Conversational, slightly vulnerable at the HOOK (the failure admission).
- Pace increases at the mechanism reveal — energy rises with the payoff.
- Can break eye contact briefly at the failure moment (authentic, not evasive).
- Forbidden: "actually" as a filler; defensive tone on the failure.

**CONTRARIAN talking-head delivery:**
- Confident, not aggressive. State the belief calmly, then dismantle it.
- Slight head tilt or smile on the HOOK line — signals courage, not anger.
- Pace is moderate. The claim is so strong it doesn't need speed.
- on-screen text: the belief being contradicted, displayed as the HOOK is spoken.

**GODIN talking-head delivery:**
- Quiet, unhurried. Storyteller energy — the audience leans in, not back.
- No list-reading cadence. Each sentence sounds improvised even if SCRIPT-driven.
- Long pauses at emotional beats.
- Forbidden: urgency, volume increases, hard CTAs.

**JOBS talking-head delivery:**
- Theatrical. Context delivered flatly; contrast delivered with a beat; reveal with a pause before speaking.
- Minimal movement. One gesture maximum — save it for the reveal.
- Forbidden: hedging, qualifiers, apologetic framing.
