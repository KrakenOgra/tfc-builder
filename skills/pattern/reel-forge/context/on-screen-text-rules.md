## On-Screen Rules
source: reel-forge/SKILL.md

**When to use on-screen text:**
- HOOK line: always. Display the HOOK's concrete specific or the CONTRARIAN belief being named.
- Key mechanism word: one overlay at the flip point ([30-45s]) to reinforce the payoff.
- CTA: always (show the action word only: "COMMENT", "SAVE", "DM").

**When to omit:**
- NAVAL SCRIPT: no on-screen text. The aphorism density must be absorbed through listening. Text overlays fragment the experience.
- Mid-SCRIPT decorative text: never. Text that paraphrases what the creator is already saying adds visual noise without adding information.
- Every line of the SCRIPT: do not caption the full SCRIPT as on-screen text — that is auto-captions (always on), not the on-screen text overlay layer.

**Timing:**
- On-screen text appears 0.5s after the spoken word it reinforces — not before (anticipation), not more than 1s after (disconnected).
- Display duration: 2-4 seconds. Long enough to read once; short enough to not become wallpaper.

**Text format:**
- Font: bold, sans-serif. One size throughout the Reel (no sizing hierarchy in on-screen text).
- Color: white with drop shadow or dark outline. Readable on any background.
- Casing: ALL CAPS for single words and short impact phrases. Title Case for full sentences.
- Position: upper third for claims; lower third for CTA only. Never centered over the creator's face.

**BOUND VOICE constraint:**
The BOUND VOICE line is a production note, not on-screen text. It appears in the ## VOICE section of the output but is never displayed in the Reel.
