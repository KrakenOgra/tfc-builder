## Forbidden Phrases
source: reel-forge/eval-report.json

These phrases must never appear in a reel-forge SCRIPT or HOOK output. Each fails the quality gate.

**"In today's fast-paced world"**
Why forbidden: Generic framing that applies to every topic and none. Fires the scroll reflex before the HOOK lands. The viewer's pattern-recognition for low-quality content is this phrase.

**"It depends"**
Why forbidden: Destroys the HOOK's concrete promise. A SCRIPT that opens with a bold claim and closes with "it depends" makes the viewer feel deceived. HORMOZI and CONTRARIAN structures collapse under hedging.

**"Inspiring" / "motivating" / "uplifting"**
Why forbidden: Emotional labels that substitute for the mechanism. Telling the viewer they will feel inspired is not a mechanism — it is a category tag. The SCRIPT must show the mechanism; the viewer assigns the emotion.

**"Game-changer" / "revolutionary" / "next-level"**
Why forbidden: Superlatives without proof. They signal low-quality content because they are used by creators without results to offer. HORMOZI voice achieves the same impact with a number: "47 clients" > "game-changing results."

**"Have you ever wondered…"**
Why forbidden: Rhetorical question opener that delays the HOOK's concrete specific. The scroll window is 3 seconds. A question that the viewer must answer before the claim lands loses the window.

**"Welcome back" / "Hey guys" / "In this video"**
Why forbidden: Platform-native framing that signals long-form YouTube content, not short-form Reel structure. Reels open on the claim, not on the setup.

**"I think" / "I feel like" / "kind of" / "sort of"**
Why forbidden: Hedging language that undermines VOICE credibility. NAVAL, HORMOZI, and JOBS voices in particular require certainty in every claim. BUILDER and CONTRARIAN can express vulnerability through specific admissions, not epistemic hedging.

**"Tips" / "tricks" / "hacks"**
Why forbidden: Category words that signal listicle format. GODIN explicitly rejects "tips." NAVAL rejects all list structure. HORMOZI uses mechanism language ("system", "sequence", "method"), not "tips."
